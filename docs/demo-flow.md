# Demo Flow

Use this flow for a portfolio walkthrough or local SaaS MVP demo. The story is: a company receives a customer quote request, the operator checks product and cost data, QuoteOps AI calculates deterministic pricing artifacts, a human reviews approval decisions, and the dashboard/reporting surfaces summarize the work.

## Safety Frame

- No AI-generated price approval is performed.
- No price table is automatically activated.
- No quote is sent to a real customer.
- No external scraping is performed, including competitor scraping.
- No payment or email flow is implemented.
- Human review is required before business use.

## Walkthrough

1. Open the app
   - Click: start the backend and frontend, then open the local frontend URL.
   - Expect: the QuoteOps AI workspace loads with navigation sections.
   - Say: "This is a portfolio MVP for deterministic pricing operations, not a production pricing engine."
   - Safety: do not show `.env` files, tokens, or real customer data.

2. Check Health / System Status
   - Click: open Overview or call `/api/health`, `/api/health/ready`, and `/api/system/status`.
   - Expect: health, readiness, system status, and OpenAPI are reachable.
   - Say: "The demo starts with operational readiness checks before any pricing workflow."
   - Safety: status responses must not expose secrets or raw database connection strings.

3. Seed demo data if needed
   - Click: Demo Tools -> Seed demo data as an admin demo user.
   - Expect: demo products, competitors, users, and pricing artifacts are created or verified.
   - Say: "The demo data is deterministic and local/demo-only."
   - Safety: use demo seed only in local or demo environments; do not call reset during a live walkthrough unless intentionally showing cleanup.

4. Review product and cost profile
   - Click: Products and Cost Profiles.
   - Expect: demo product records and active cost profiles are visible.
   - Say: "Pricing starts from structured product cost data, not from an LLM guess."
   - Safety: do not enter real supplier costs during a public demo.

5. Create or inspect customer quote request
   - Click: Customer Requests.
   - Expect: a request captures customer intent, quantity, and review status.
   - Say: "Quote requests give the operator a work queue before pricing decisions are made."
   - Safety: no quote is sent to a real customer from this MVP.

6. Generate quote preview
   - Click: Quote Preview and select a product/quantity.
   - Expect: deterministic unit price, total price, margin, and notes.
   - Say: "The quote preview uses active table data and backend formulas."
   - Safety: previewing a quote does not approve, send, or activate anything.

7. Generate candidate prices
   - Click: Candidate Prices.
   - Expect: candidate rows show deterministic pricing options for strategy review.
   - Say: "Candidate prices are generated by backend business rules."
   - Safety: candidate generation does not become an active price table automatically.

8. Run price validation
   - Click: Price Validation.
   - Expect: validation status, risk level, and warnings such as margin or progression risks.
   - Say: "Validation helps catch risky pricing before a manager reviews it."
   - Safety: validation is advisory; human review is still required.

9. Submit approval request
   - Click: Approval Requests and create or inspect a pending request.
   - Expect: a request is pending with reviewer context and decision notes.
   - Say: "The workflow separates calculated recommendations from human decision authority."
   - Safety: submitting approval does not activate a price table by itself.

10. Approve or reject through human workflow
    - Click: Approve or Reject as a manager/admin demo user.
    - Expect: status changes and review metadata are recorded.
    - Say: "Only a human role can approve or reject; AI does not approve prices."
    - Safety: approval workflow is demo-local and does not send customer communications.

11. Review audit logs
    - Click: Audit Logs.
    - Expect: actions such as demo views, approval decisions, and report generation are listed.
    - Say: "Audit logs make pricing operations explainable after the fact."
    - Safety: logs should not contain secrets, tokens, or password hashes.

12. Run pricing simulation
    - Click: Pricing Simulations.
    - Expect: before/after scenario metrics show deterministic trade-offs.
    - Say: "Simulation compares pricing choices before using them in a customer workflow."
    - Safety: simulation does not modify active price tables.

13. Create scenario comparison
    - Click: Scenario Comparisons.
    - Expect: side-by-side scenario rows with margin, total price, gross profit, and risk labels.
    - Say: "This helps compare multiple deterministic pricing scenarios."
    - Safety: comparison is analysis only; it does not approve or activate prices.

14. View KPI dashboard
    - Click: Dashboard.
    - Expect: quote, approval, validation, pricing, workflow, and audit metrics.
    - Say: "The dashboard summarizes pricing operations for an operator or manager."
    - Safety: dashboard metrics are summaries, not automated business actions.

15. View dashboard insights
    - Click: Dashboard Insights.
    - Expect: deterministic insight rules explain items that need attention.
    - Say: "Insights are rule-based explanations of operational signals."
    - Safety: insights do not generate or approve prices.

16. Generate HTML report
    - Click: HTML Reports and generate a report for a supported artifact.
    - Expect: a safe HTML report is generated for review.
    - Say: "Reports package deterministic results for human review."
    - Safety: reports must not include secrets, unsafe scripts, or raw tracebacks.

17. Explain safety boundaries
    - Click: Demo Tools -> Demo Guide, or close with the safety frame above.
    - Expect: the audience understands what the MVP does and does not do.
    - Say: "QuoteOps AI assists pricing operations, but human review remains the control point."
    - Safety: do not claim autonomous approval, production deployment, scraping, payment, or email features.
